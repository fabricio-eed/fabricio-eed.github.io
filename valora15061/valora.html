Valora - PROJ 15061

Context:

"Valora, S.A. is a banknote printing works fully owned by Banco de Portugal. (...) Valora produces the share of euro banknotes allocated to Banco de Portugal by the Eurosystem. In the Eurosystem, banknote production is decentralised: every national central bank is responsible for producing one or more denominations annually, either for internal issue or to supply other central banks. (...) Banknote production by Valora also complies fully with demanding international standards, including those defined by the European Central Bank for the production of euro banknotes. Production by Valora is certified in accordance with the international Quality, Environmental and Safety Standards, the ISO 9001, ISO 14001 and OSHAS 18001 respectively" (source: www.bportugal.pt/valora-sa)

Developer and system integrator for Valora. The requirements and the level of proficiency and rigor are strongly necessary to ensure work efficiency (OEE), machine safety, and proof of theft. In 2017 they produced 144 millions of banknotes. Euro banknotes produced by Valora are easily identifiable through the serial number, which always starts with M. (source: www.bportugal.pt/valora-sa)

Tasks:

Two Robot ensures the movement for pick, vision scanning, process of labelling, then checking the label correct placement and place on the corresponding conveyor. The third, if necessary assist the opening of a cardboard box and place the wad of notes inside. Again, if necessary the robot assist in the closing of the box.

The Vision system performs various tasks along the assembly line. It checks the serial number of the wad, confirms the labelling with success and checks the content of the packing box, id number and the proper placement of the security adhesive tape.
